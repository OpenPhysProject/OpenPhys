Happy Happy X-ray
X-rays can lose energy and change direction.

Compton

In Compton X-ray scattering, also known as the Compton effect, an X-ray photon can lose a large fraction of its energy in a single collision. The incident X-ray photon is scattered by a free electron, which recoils. This recoil electron may receive a large KE, and so go on to produce many ionization events. The scattered photon has less energy, and so longer wavelength, than the incident photon.

Radiotherapy

In radiotherapy X-rays are used to kill tumors. Compton X-ray scattering is the most important effect leading to absorbed dose in external beam radiotherapy. The recoil electron has high kinetic energy, which results in ionization and excitation, leading to chemical damage and cell death.
